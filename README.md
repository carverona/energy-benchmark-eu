### European Energy Benchmark - Nature Dashboard

The core of this exploration is to quantify the impact that solar and wind farms have on nature. 

"Impact on nature" is defined local terrestrial biodiversity intactness, expressed by the mean species abundance (MSA) indicator, 
as a function of six human pressures: land use, road disturbance, fragmentation, hunting, atmospheric nitrogen deposition and climate 
change. The MSA is based on a comparison between the current state of an ecosystem and its original or 'reference' state. The value of the MSA varies on a scale from 0 to 100, where 100 indicates an undisturbed ecosystem, in which species abundance is comparable to the natural state, while 0 represents an ecosystem that is completely degraded or in which the original species have disappeared. 

To know more about MSA, read here: https://www.globio.info/what-is-globio

Approach: retrieving coordinates through OpenStreetMap server, quantifying their surface area, and matching them to a specialized energy dataset

Result: more than 600 individual wind farms and 200 solar farms mapped in europe.
